Experience



Electrical Subteam - McMaster RoboSub


As a member of the McMaster RoboSub team, I contributed to the development of the autonomous underwater vehicle by engaging in both hands-on hardware implementation and in-depth technical research. I selected and validated an appropriate Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU) for underwater navigation, gaining practical experience with sensors like the MPU6050 and simulating sensor behavior using Wokwi. I also explored waterproofing strategies critical for submersion, including the use of O-rings, compression cable glands, and heat-shrinking tubing. On the electrical side, I designed and laid out custom PCBs and schematics in Altium Designer, ensuring integration with the broader system architecture. I worked on simulating Doppler Velocity Log (DVL) behavior using Arduino IDE to support underwater localization. Additionally, I conducted extensive research into Controller Area Network (CAN bus) protocols, producing a detailed implementation plan to optimize communication between microcontrollers and onboard devices. This experience gave me a strong foundation in underwater robotics, embedded systems, and real-world engineering collaboration.



Electrical Subteam - McMaster Aerial Robotics and Drones Club

RAG-Based Chatbot with Local Knowledge - Individual



I created a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) chatbot capable of answering questions based on custom uploaded documents. Using sentence transformers to generate embeddings and FAISS for efficient similarity search, the system retrieved relevant text chunks which were passed to an LLM (llama 2 - ran it on ollama) for context-aware responses. This project sharpened my understanding of embedding models, vector databases, and grounding large language models in external knowledge sources.




Hand Proximity Detection with OpenCV + TTS - Individual



This computer vision project used OpenCV to identify hand positions and nearby objects in real-time video. By calculating the minimum distance between detected hand contours and object contours, the system determined which hand was closer to which object. A text-to-speech (TTS) module then vocally announced the results. Through this project, I gained experience in contour detection, real-time video processing, spatial analysis, and integrating audio feedback with computer vision pipelines.
